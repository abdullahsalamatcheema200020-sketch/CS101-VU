Chapter 2: History of Computing

A time-travel journey through 2000+ years of computer evolution! ⏳

The Amazing Journey

Computers didn't just appear overnight. Great minds worked for thousands of years to build the computer you're using right now!

🧮
~3000 BC

The Abacus

The very first "computer"! A wooden frame with beads used for counting. Still used today in some schools. No electricity needed! ⚡

⚙️
1642 - 1800s

Gear Technology

Pascal made a gear-based calculator. Leibniz improved it. Charles Babbage designed the "Analytical Engine" — the first computer design! His friend Ada Lovelace wrote the first algorithm, making her the first programmer! 👩‍💻

🎫
1890

Punch Cards

Herman Hollerith used cards with holes to store data. His company later became IBM — one of the biggest tech companies! 🏢

💡
1946

ENIAC — The Giant!

The first electronic computer! It was HUGE — 1800 square feet (bigger than a house! 🏠), weighed 27 tons, and had 17,000 vacuum tubes. It could do what your phone does in a millisecond!

🔬
1950s - 1970s

Transistors & Chips

Transistors replaced huge vacuum tubes, making computers smaller. Then came microchips — the tiny brains in every modern device. Intel was born! 🧠

💻
1980s - Today

Personal Computers & Beyond

Apple and Microsoft made computers for homes. Then came Internet, smartphones, AI, and now... the future belongs to YOU! 🚀

Fun Facts!

🤯 The first computer programmer was a woman — Ada Lovelace (1843)!
🐛 The word "bug" (for computer error) came from a real insect stuck inside a computer in 1947!
📏 Your smartphone is millions of times more powerful than ENIAC (which filled a room)!

Key Takeaways

✅ Abacus = First counting tool (3000 BC)

✅ Babbage = Father of computing (Analytical Engine)

✅ Ada Lovelace = First programmer

✅ ENIAC = First electronic computer (1946)

✅ Computers evolved from huge → tiny → pocket-sized
